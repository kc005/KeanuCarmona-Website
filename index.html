Fund Management

All my life I have been interested in finance, trading, and the cyclical flow of the modern economy. Although the experience I have in finance is limited to personal investments, my curiosity in financial analytics has grown insurmountably. Just browsing blogs and newsletters, I find myself intrigued with the content: fueled by curious passion. In my financial incipience, the guidance and training at any notable financial organization would bolster my abilities and allow me to better prove myself in a professional and competitive financial environment.

There was an instance towards the end of my first quarter of college where I had a stock option expiry date during a class final, where I had to do an impromptu valuation of the collegiate value of the final and compared it to the monetary value of the option. The course was important to my degree so I decided on liquidating the option early and taking my final on time.

There was a time while in college where I was involved in a group project with two others. Halfway through the project, one of the group members was having trouble staying on top of the deadlines and so, to introduce some incentive, I introduced her to investing and told her to contact me whenever she had any questions. Then, once she began asking questions about stocks to invest in, I told her I'd find her some growing stocks while she worked on the assignment. This made the monetary value of a good investment directly related to her progress on the assignment, and she finished her portion of the assignment with a new sense of urgency.
